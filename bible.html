The Music Bible

The Ten Commandments of Music (KVV)

  1. Thou shalt not mumble excessively.
  2. Thou shalt honor thy producer and thy engineer.
  3. Thou shalt not spell thy name absurdly.
  4. Thou shalt not covet thy neighbor’s bars.
  5. Thou shalt not fake streams.
  6. Thou shalt commit no lyrical filler.
  7. Thou shalt always respect the Old School.
  8. Thou shalt not bear false flexes.
  9. Thou shalt always address the mic with confidence.
  10. Thou shalt keep thy flows fresh.

Proverbs of the Plug (KVV)

1. He who moveth bricks in broad daylight, yet rappeth on wax the details thereof, hath surely summoned the feds.

2. Blessed is the plug who counteth money in silence, for noise bringeth unwanted eyes and ears.

3. Thou shalt not speaketh on crimes thou hast not committed, nor flex funds thou hast not counted. Such foolishness beareth witness only to thy corny nature.

4. Better a humble bag secured quietly than a loud flex undone by truth.

5. Whosoever rappeth loudly of bodies caught but hath not shed blood, speaketh lies into existence and invites wrath upon his head.

6. A wise plug speaketh little but grindeth much; fools boast loudly yet grindeth none.

7. Thy chain shineth falsely if thy pockets remaineth empty; thus saith the street.

8. He who fabricates the flex in rhymes shall see his reputation perish swiftly, like ashes in the wind.

9. True power cometh from moves made in shadows; beware the rapper who claimeth daylight deeds.

10. The path of snitches is paved with verses too vivid and confessions too clear.

11. Guard thy mouth as diligently as thou guardest thy stash; words spill quicker than secrets.

12. A wise man grindeth discreetly and stacketh silently; his flex shall speak in due season.

13. Respecteth the old heads who paved paths; their wisdom spareth thee from folly.

14. Thou shalt never bite the hand of thy plug; loyalty bringeth greater dividends than betrayal.

15. Money laundered clean remaineth green; speak not of the wash lest thou be cleansed by judgment.

16. Speaketh not the specifics of thy hustle; vague truths protecteth freedoms.

17. Beware those who count clout above cash; vanity hastens downfall.

18. Flexes not lived are borrowed garments—bright today, returned tomorrow.

19. Maintaineth distance from loud-mouth steppas; their talk inviteth tragedy.

20. The wise plug investeth in silence and patience; his stacks groweth while others diminish.

21. Verily, a quiet trap is blessed; noise attracteth vultures.

22. Boasteth not of things undone; thy reputation is thy currency.

23. Those who rap about riches unseen bring mockery upon their names.

24. The corny rapper shouteth loudest; real ones whisper and prevail.

25. Thou shalt not follow trends blindly; originality giveth longevity.

26. Rappeth not of false wars; thy lies shall crumble thy castle swiftly.

27. Discretion in dealings yieldeth endless bounty; reckless tongues reap scarcity.

28. Claimeth not a gang thou knowest not; colors falsely flown leadeth swiftly to shame.

29. Blessed is he who grindeth with caution, for his days on earth shall multiply.

30. The wise plug chooseth carefully his allies; snakes cometh in all forms.
